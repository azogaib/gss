THE HISTORY OF GSS

GSS Founder Is Inspired in the Mid 1970s

While working in direct sales selling lightbulbs, Global Sensor Systems’ founder Gordan Cooper was inspired by the beeping sound of a truck backing up to a loading dock. He called his brother, Richard, to explain his idea of creating a similar product. Richard has a background in electronics, and the pair knew they could come up with a similar product.

Gordon Searches for Product Users

After researching potential users for their new product, Gordon discovered a company called Disposal Services in Weston. He met with Harry Chad, who was in charge of the company’s trucks, and explained his interest in the truck’s backup beeping devices. Harry replied, “It’s not the people around the vehicle who are the problem. I need something that will actually stop the truck from backing into sh*t!”

The Lightbulb Goes Off in Gordon’s Head

After leaving Harry’s office, Gordon went home and watched a show called Longstreet, in which the main character uses a cane that vibrated when it came in close proximity to an object. That’s when the lightbulb that sparked GSS’ sensor system went off in Gordon’s head.

Gordon Creates the Draft of His Concept

By 1977, Gordon created rough drafts of his idea. He shared the concept with Richard and their father, Charles. The trio spent a year designing, building, and testing the product in their garage.

Gordon Begins Selling His Systems

Gordon started selling his product to shop mechanics and did demonstrations. This eventually led him to meet fleet managers and owners.

GSS was Founded

In 1986, GSS was established. It became the solution to help prevent the common backing accidents occurring in the waste management industry. GSS began working with clients like the City of Toronto.

The Future of GSS

Since its founding, GSS’ team has grown. In 2022, Gordon’s daughter, Caroline, joined her family’s business. GSS now serves a range of distributors throughout North America, selling their commercial vehicle safety systems.

EMPLOYMENT OPPORTUNITIES

Operations Coordinator

Global Sensor Systems is searching for an Operations Coordinator to join our growing team!

The Role:

Reporting to the Director of Operations, the Operations Coordinator will be responsible for:

  • Assembling products (Kits/Systems) to be shipped to customers
  • Packing, shipping, and receiving parts and kits
  • Manage the inventory using the Shopify platform and Materials Inventory app
  • Ensure accurate and real-time statuses on all customer orders
  • Liaise with suppliers, ordering parts as required
  • Process purchase orders in partnership with the Finance team
  • Process returns and warranty
  • Support customers with installation
  • Create/update installation manuals and documents in partnership with Marketing
  • Trouble shoot breakdowns and repairs with customers either by phone or in person
  • Assist distributors with set up and installations

The ideal candidate:

  • Must be a self-starter who has experience working with automotive and/or electrical parts
  • A minimum of 2 years’ experience in a technical field within the automotive industry preferred
  • Experience using third party platforms and apps such as Shopify and Materials Inventory or similar
  • Prior experience in logistics including shipping, receiving, and inventory control systems
  • Manual dexterity and the ability to perform repetitive tasks is required
  • Must have ability to safely lift up to 40 lbs. on a frequent basis
  • Thrive in a fast-paced environment, reliable and has a keen sense of urgency
  • Responsive to deadlines and comfortable managing multiple priorities
  • Extremely detailed and excellent organizational skills
  • Self-motivated and disciplined to work with minimal supervision
  • Excellent communication skills, both written and verbal
  • Valid G license

Location: Goreway Drive, Mississauga, Ontario

How to Apply: Please forward your cover letter with resume and include Operations Coordinator in the subject line. We thank you for your interest, however, only qualified candidates will be contacted.
